Spaghetti Carbonara

A classic Italian pasta dish with a creamy egg-based sauce, crispy bacon, and Parmesan cheese. Simple yet elegant!

‚è±Ô∏è Prep: 10 mins üî• Cook: 15 mins Medium

Ingredients (Serves 4)

  • 400g spaghetti
  • 200g bacon or pancetta, diced
  • 4 large eggs
  • 100g Parmesan cheese, grated
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to boil. Cook spaghetti according to package directions until al dente.
  2. While pasta cooks, fry bacon in a large pan over medium heat until crispy. Add garlic and cook for 1 minute.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together eggs and Parmesan cheese. Season with black pepper.
  4. Reserve 1 cup of pasta water, then drain spaghetti.
  5. Remove pan from heat. Add hot spaghetti to bacon and toss.
  6. Quickly pour in egg mixture while tossing constantly. Add pasta water gradually to create a creamy sauce.
  7. Serve immediately with extra Parmesan and parsley.

Tips & Notes

  • Temperature: Make sure to remove pan from heat before adding eggs to prevent scrambling.
  • Cheese: Use freshly grated Parmesan for best results.
  • Variations: Add peas or mushrooms for extra vegetables.
  • Storage: Best served fresh, but leftovers can be refrigerated for 1-2 days.

Fluffy Pancakes

Light, fluffy, and delicious breakfast pancakes that are perfect for lazy weekend mornings!

‚è±Ô∏è Prep: 10 mins üî• Cook: 10 mins Easy

Ingredients (Makes 8-10)

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt

  • 1¬æ cups milk
  • ¬º cup melted butter
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Mix flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt in a large bowl.
  2. In another bowl, whisk eggs, milk, melted butter, and vanilla.
  3. Pour wet ingredients into dry ingredients and stir until just combined (don't overmix!).
  4. Heat a griddle or pan over medium heat and lightly grease.
  5. Pour ¬º cup batter for each pancake.
  6. Cook until bubbles form on surface, then flip and cook until golden.
  7. Serve warm with maple syrup and butter!

Tips & Notes

  • Fluffiness: Don't overmix the batter - lumps are okay!
  • Temperature: Medium heat is key - too hot and they'll burn.
  • Toppings: Try fresh berries, whipped cream, or chocolate chips.

Chocolate Brownies

Rich, fudgy chocolate brownies with a crackly top and gooey center. Pure chocolate heaven!

‚è±Ô∏è Prep: 15 mins üî• Bake: 25 mins Easy

Ingredients (9x9 pan)

  • ¬Ω cup butter, melted
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 2 eggs
  • ‚Öì cup cocoa powder
  • ¬Ω cup flour
  • ¬º teaspoon salt
  • ¬º teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Optional: chocolate chips or nuts

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 350¬∞F (175¬∞C). Grease and line a 9x9 inch pan.
  2. Mix melted butter and sugar until well combined.
  3. Beat in eggs one at a time, then add vanilla.
  4. Sift in cocoa powder, flour, salt, and baking powder. Mix until just combined.
  5. Fold in chocolate chips or nuts if using.
  6. Pour batter into prepared pan and spread evenly.
  7. Bake for 20-25 minutes until edges are set but center is slightly soft.
  8. Cool completely before cutting into squares.

Tips & Notes

  • Fudgy vs Cakey: For fudgier brownies, slightly underbake them.
  • Testing: Insert a toothpick - it should have moist crumbs, not be clean.
  • Storage: Keep in airtight container for up to 4 days.
  • Pro tip: Let cool completely for clean cuts!
